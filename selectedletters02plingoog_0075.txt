Pliny explains why the legacy just left him by Curianus had given him
so much pleasure; namely, because it was a recompensa to him for
having formerly acted with equity and generosity towards Curianus, when
the latter had been disinherited by his mother Pomponia in favour of
Pliny, Severus and others.
